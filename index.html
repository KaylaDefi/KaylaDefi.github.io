About Me

It started with curiosity—how a few lines of code could bring an idea to life. That curiosity turned into late nights with code, an ever-growing love for full-stack development, and a mild obsession with cryptography. Today, I’m exploring the world of blockchain—how decentralized tech can shift power, protect privacy, and occasionally break my brain (in the best way).
As a certified software developer, I’ve worked across the full stack, and I’m especially drawn to technologies that enable transparency, security, and autonomy. I enjoy diving into concepts like universally composable security, zero-knowledge proofs, and decentralized storage. I’ve worked across the MERN stack, and I’m currently building dApps with Next.js and integrating tools like Anchor and Rust. Through my blog, "Dev Logs from the Edge", I document my ongoing journey of curiousity—experimenting, learning, and pushing boundaries one project at a time.
I am open to collaborations and embrace new opportunities that challenge me to grow further. I love hearing new ideas and discussing theory. Please feel free to reach out to me with any questions, comments, or feedback.
