About Me

It started with curiosity, how a few lines of code could bring an idea to life. That curiosity grew into late nights debugging, a deep love for full-stack development, and an ongoing fascination with cryptography and blockchain systems.
As a certified software developer, I focus on building transparent and secure applications. I’m certified in the MERN stack but work fluidly across a variety of tools and frameworks. Lately, I’ve been developing dApps with Next.js and integrating Rust and Anchor on Solana, while exploring topics like universally composable security, zero-knowledge proofs, and decentralized storage architectures.
My blog, Dev Logs from the Edge, chronicles my journey as I learn, experiment, and push boundaries, whether I’m writing smart contracts, analyzing key management protocols, or breaking things in local testnets (on purpose, mostly).
Always open to collaboration and conversation, feel free to reach out with questions, ideas, or comments.
